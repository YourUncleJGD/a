2. Resource Concentration Risk

Production of key battery components is geographically concentrated, posing supply chain and geopolitical risks.

  • Lithium (Li): 75% from Top 3 Countries
  • Cobalt (Co): 70% from One Country (DRC)
  • Nickel (Ni): 60% Used in high-density cathodes

3. Consequences & Mitigation

  • Carbon Footprint: Manufacturing typically accounts for 50-70% of a battery's lifetime CO2 emissions.
  • Water Usage: Lithium extraction can require 1.9 million liters of water per ton of Lithium carbonate.
  • E-Waste Challenge: The current global battery recycling rate for consumer electronics is estimated to be below 5%.

4. Scientific Innovation: The Circular Economy

  • Advanced Recycling: New hydrometallurgy processes target 95%+ recovery of high-value metals (Ni, Co, Cu).
  • Second Life: Used EV batteries, still holding 70-80% capacity, are redeployed for grid storage (a non-mobile application).
  • Alternative Chemistry: Sodium-ion batteries eliminate Cobalt and Lithium, relying on vastly more abundant resources.
